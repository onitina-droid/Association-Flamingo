BRIEF PRESENTATION

The Flamingo Association works in the fields of education, digital inclusion, and community development. It has led numerous initiatives to support girls and women, particularly through STEM and digital skills training programs to foster their empowerment and professional integration.
Convinced that education and technology are essential levers for social transformation, the Flamingo Association works to empower girls, improve their future prospects, promote equal opportunities, and defend their rights in a constantly changing world.

The Flamingo Association began in 2020 as the vision of two high school girls in Madagascar who believed in equal opportunities for girls in STEM. Determined to break gender stereotypes, they launched initiatives in STEM education, workshops, and mentorship. What started as a teenage dream is now a movement that has impacted over 200 girls, supported by a team of 30 volunteers, mostly young women. Today, Flamingo continues to transform lives, equipping girls across Madagascar with the skills and opportunities to thrive in the digital and scientific fields.

OUR VALUES

Education

We believe that education is the key to change. By providing STEM and digital skills, we empower young girls and women to build a better future.

Inclusion

Every girl deserves the same opportunities, regardless of her background or circumstances. We strive to create an accessible and equitable environment where everyone can thrive.

Solidarity

We believe in the power of collective action. By working together, we support each other and strengthen our community, ensuring that no one is left behind in the pursuit of a brighter future.

Projects 2024-2025

Coding for all 2024

Lycee Kanana , Antsirabe, Madagascar

Coding for All 2024 is an inclusive program offering coding and digital skills to both girls and boys, including those with disabilities. One student with a hearing impairment, accompanied by friends who translate for him, benefits from tailored support. The program ensures equal opportunities, fostering skill development through hands-on projects.

STEAMINIST

Alliance , Antsirabe

STEMinist 2024 is a 5-day hackathon and workshop designed for college and high school girls in Madagascar. The event features exciting challenges in robotics, coding, and environmental sustainability, encouraging participants to collaborate, innovate, and develop practical solutions. This initiative aims to inspire young girls to explore STEM careers and build confidence in their skills.

Our two founder's history

Onitina Raharimalala

The Founder

Onitina Raharimalala is a passionate advocate for digital education and gender equality in STEM. As the founder of Association Flamingo, she is dedicated to empowering young girls and women in Madagascar through technology and innovation. With a background in telecommunications and IT, Onitina has led impactful initiatives such as Girls Can Code, STEMinist Hackathon, and Ell’volution, helping participants gain digital skills and access career opportunities in the tech industry. Her leadership fosters a supportive community where women and girls, especially from underrepresented backgrounds, can thrive in STEM fields.!

Voary Manjaka

The Co-founder

Voary Manjaka is the co-founder of Association Flamingo, working alongside Onitina Raharimalala to advance digital education and STEM opportunities for young girls and women in Madagascar. Passionate about technology, education, and gender equality, Voary plays a key role in shaping the organization’s programs, such as Girls Can Code and the STEMinist Hackathon. Her dedication to empowering underrepresented communities through digital skills and innovation has made a significant impact, helping many participants pursue careers in tech and gain financial independence.
